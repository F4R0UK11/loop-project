# Current-State Journey Map — How institutional knowledge breaks down and rebuilds through people

Persona A "the confused / lost student or faculty member." Composite of Youssef, Mehdi, and Mohammed · AUI UX Bootcamp.

## The six stages (with emotional arc)

### 1. Trigger — an unfamiliar process appears (Neutral)
- **Doing:** Encounters a process for the first time — can hit at any point in an AUI career. Youssef (week 1): class locations. Mehdi: Add/Drop. Mohammed (junior): exchange.
- **Thinking:** "This must be documented somewhere." "Where's Add/Drop on the portal?" "Where is this class?" "I need to go on exchange — now what?"
- **Feeling:** Neutral to mildly uneasy. Not yet worried — assumes the answer is one search away.
- **Touchpoints:** Class schedule, student portal, overheard conversations.
- **Pain point:** The trigger is often time-sensitive (a class starting, a registration window, a document deadline); there's no slack to "figure it out."

### 2. Initial search — tries official channels first (Hopeful)
- **Doing:** Checks the obvious official source. Youssef goes to Admissions/Administration. Mehdi digs through the student portal. Mohammed looks for a defined exchange process.
- **Thinking:** "There has to be a clear procedure for this — it's a big university."
- **Feeling:** Still hopeful, slightly rushed.
- **Touchpoints:** Admissions office (in person), student portal, official website.
- **Pain point:** The information "technically exists" but isn't surfaced where they're looking — it's scattered across offices, platforms, and people's heads.

### 3. Hits a wall — official channel falls short (Frustrated)
- **Doing:** Gets an incomplete, inconsistent, or missing answer. Youssef says AUI offices "often don't give a complete or accurate answer."
- **Thinking:** "Even the office I went to didn't really know / didn't tell me everything."
- **Feeling:** Frustrated, sometimes a little embarrassed for not already knowing.
- **Touchpoints:** Admin office staff, portal (no clear path), assumed-but-unstated rules.
- **Pain point:** Real cost: Mehdi's $200/week storage fine, Youssef's curfew violation and DSA questioning — both because no one surfaced the rule in time.

### 4. Turns to people — peer network activates (Anxious)
- **Doing:** Falls back on informal channels — asks a Mentor, a friend, an upperclassman, or checks the Add/Drop Facebook group. Mohammed tracks down Sidra, someone who'd already done the exact exchange.
- **Thinking:** "Someone who's already been through this will just tell me."
- **Feeling:** Relief (there's a path forward) mixed with anxiety (dependent on one person responding).
- **Touchpoints:** Mentor or friend, Add/Drop Facebook group, WhatsApp/Instagram threads.
- **Pain point:** No single trustworthy channel. The Facebook group "isn't 100% trustworthy" per Youssef. Success depends on knowing the right specific upperclassman.

### 5. Resolution — gets the real answer (Relieved)
- **Doing:** Receives the actual, practical answer — often a piece of tacit knowledge never written down.
- **Thinking:** "Why didn't anyone just tell me this on day one?"
- **Feeling:** Relief, sometimes tinged with frustration at how long it took.
- **Touchpoints:** The trusted person (Mentor, upperclassman), tacit knowledge passed person-to-person.
- **Pain point:** The answer lives in a single person, not in any searchable system. "Choose the professor, don't choose the time." — advice from Youssef.

### 6. Becomes the source — now they're the one being asked (Burdened)
- **Doing:** Having lived through it, they become a go-to answer source for the next student: exchange steps, undrop procedures, Add/Drop tricks.
- **Thinking:** "I already answered this exact question for someone else last week."
- **Feeling:** Mildly burdened but willing — a sense of informal duty, not institutional responsibility.
- **Touchpoints:** Word of mouth, direct messages, being "that person" for a specific topic.
- **Pain point:** The next cohort starts the loop over at Stage 1. This knowledge lives only in them. It isn't logged, searchable, or transferable. When a student graduates or a faculty member moves on from being "new," it's gone.

## Pain Points & Opportunities (ranked by severity and strategic leverage)

| # | Pain Point | Opportunity |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | No single trustworthy source (Stages 2-4): official channels are incomplete, peer channels are unverified | Surface incomplete processes early: flag known-incomplete-answer processes before the student hits the wall |
| 2 | Rules discovered only after being broken (Stage 3): real financial / disciplinary cost | Proactive rule surfacing: surface unstated rules at the moment they become relevant, not after |
| 3 | Second-impression problem (Stages 2-4): asking again carries social cost, especially for non-freshmen | Structure peer knowledge: give the peer-knowledge channel structure and verification |
| 4 | Knowledge decay at graduation (Stage 6): the answer exists briefly in a person, then vanishes | Capture at the moment of learning: capture the real answer the instant someone learns it |

## Key Insight
The highest-leverage moment is Stage 5→6: the instant someone learns the real answer is exactly when it should be captured for the next person, instead of relying on them to informally become "the source."

**Break the loop by institutionalizing tacit knowledge at the moment of discovery.**
