Assignment 3 — Crazy 8s and Competitor Analysis
Institutional Knowledge Doesn't Scale — AUI UX Bootcamp

Part A: Crazy 8s
Eight different directions, sketched fast, on purpose kept different from each other rather than eight versions of the same idea. The starred one (the super-app) is the team's main direction.
1. Super-app (Social + AI)  ★ main idea
One app in every pocket. The AI feeds off the social feed plus the different departments' APIs, so students get both peer answers and official info in a single place.
2. WhatsApp Community
One big WhatsApp group with subgroups for housing, SSE, business, and so on, so questions land with the people who actually know that specific area.
3. Live Student Line
A real person you call who knows the campus and guides you through whatever you're stuck on, in the moment.
4. Official Reddit
An official AUI Reddit where students can ask anything, and search past answers with Reddit's AI so the same question doesn't have to be re-asked every year.
5. Campus Kiosks
Interactive kiosks around campus for maps, course-code lookup, and the live busyness of study spots and cafes.
6. AUI GPT
An official bot trained on the handbook. Policies only, always correct, so there's one trustworthy source for the official rules.
7. Living Handbook
A handbook 2.0 written by students, for students. Real, practical tips layered on top of the official policies.
8. Verified Peer Match
Matched to someone who lived your exact case, based on your situation (a new idea from this round).

What this shows: nothing on this list is both AUI-specific and built to survive past a single cohort. The peer channels (mentors, group chats) are the only ones that actually hold the right kind of knowledge, but they're the least durable. The most durable channels (handbook, LMS) hold the least useful kind of knowledge. That gap (AUI-specific, situation-scoped, low social cost, and durable across graduating classes) is where an opportunity sits, and it's what the super-app (concept 1), the living handbook (concept 7), and the verified peer match (concept 8) above are aimed at directly.
