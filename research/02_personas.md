# Personas — Institutional Knowledge Doesn't Scale (AUI UI/UX Bootcamp)

## Persona A (PRIMARY) — Yassine, "The confused / lost community member"
- **Status:** Student or Faculty · **Age:** Varies · **Major/Dept:** Varies · **Location:** Ifrane, Morocco
- **Quotes:** "Choose the professor, don't choose the time." / "I just didn't know I needed to register my car with campus security."
- **Goals:**
  - Get a fast, accurate answer to a specific, timely question
  - Know a rule or deadline before it costs something
  - Avoid tracking down one specific person and hoping they reply
- **Needs:**
  - One low-friction place to ask a question — academic or logistical
  - A reliable, current answer without needing an existing network
- **Frustrations:**
  - Info exists somewhere at AUI but is scattered across portal, offices, people's heads
  - Admin answers are often incomplete or inconsistent
  - No single trustworthy channel — faculty don't even have a fallback group
- **Tools:** Facebook, Mentor, Outlook, Canvas, HR Portal
- **Note:** Persona A represents both students and faculty facing something for the first time — e.g. a new professor relocating to Ifrane with family faces the same access problem in a different form (schools, groceries, HR).

## Persona B (SECONDARY) — Salma, "The knowledgeable senior / mentor"
- **Status:** Student Mentor · **Age:** 22 · **Major:** Software Systems Eng. · **Location:** Ifrane, Morocco
- **Quote:** "It's better to get multiple perspectives than just ask one mentor because it could be subjective."
- **Goals:**
  - Share what she knows once, reaching more than one student
  - Be recognized as reliable without being expected to be infallible
  - Correct misinformation before it spreads further
- **Needs:**
  - A way to contribute an answer once and have it resurface automatically
  - A lightweight way to flag or update info that turns out wrong
- **Frustrations:**
  - Answers the same 2-3 questions repeatedly, one person at a time
  - Treated as the single source of truth on high-stakes topics
  - No formal channel credits or scales what she already does for free
- **Tools:** Facebook Groups, AUI Confessions, Reddit, Quora, Substack

## Persona C (EXTREME) — Karim, "Visiting professor / temporary staff"
- **Status:** Visiting Faculty, 1 yr · **Age:** 41 · **Dept:** Physics · **Location:** Ifrane, Morocco
- **Quote:** "I know my field. I don't know this town, and no one's built anything for someone like me to learn it fast."
- **Goals:**
  - Find reliable, non-student-specific local knowledge quickly
  - Avoid costly, avoidable mistakes from unwritten local rules
  - Settle his family with minimal disruption
- **Needs:**
  - An entry point into local knowledge that doesn't assume he's a student
  - A channel that doesn't require an existing peer network
- **Frustrations:**
  - Every "everyone knows this" fact must be rediscovered from zero
  - Shorter stay means barely any time to build a network
  - Nearly all informal channels are built for students, not staff
- **Tools:** Staff Email, HR Portal, Word of mouth
- **Note:** Persona C is a reasoned extrapolation (no direct interview data) — useful for pressure-testing whether the concept holds beyond student life.
